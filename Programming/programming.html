Programming Stuff

After a lot of thought, I decided to buy an evaluation board for myself. After reading through various boards, I finally cornered down to Wandboard from Freescale. I downloaded wandboards uBoot, Linux kernel and used busybox for rootFS. This is a brief walk-through of the things I did to get 'prompt'.
Their official website: http://www.wandboard.org/

The first step to getting anything running in wandboard to is to buy yourself a wandboard. I bought mine from Future electronics, its around 10 USD less than else where, and in my experience the support stuff were really patient to tolerate my annoyances. Moreover they ship anywhere across the globe. So I recommend get it from them. (Suggestions as if it were a million dollar Lear jet..!)
Here is their link (for those who are lazy enough to search it out in google :P): http://www.futureelectronics.com/WebsiteLanding.aspx

1. Search for wandboard after entering their website.
2. Last information I heard from them is that wandboard is a high demand product. So you may expect some delays in getting yours. I got mine when few people knew about it. So it was shipped quickly.
3. They make ask you to fill up, sign and send back some forms, since its a restricted product. Don't be surprised if you see any ridiculous questions in the form.

Obviously, there are tonnes of blogs explaining this procedure. And I thought why not add one more to the list, this one being my crappy website. :P

I categorize it into three parts:

  • U-Boot
  • Linux Kernel
  • BusyBox
Before going into any of these make sure you have a cross compiler installed in your host. Follow this CROSS_COMPILE to do the installation, if you do not have one installed.
